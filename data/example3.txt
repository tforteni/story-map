Alina had always loved the smell of rain. As a child growing up in Rivenfell, where the rains were as constant as night and day, as growth and death, she took a special sort of comfort in the rain. Callahan had no such constancy. The northern state had no seasons by which to mark the time, no earthly rhythms to teach children patience and adults hope. There were only the capricious whims of the climate. Alina found that she loved Callahan even more. When her father travelled north from Rivenfell to Callahan it took him three weeks and for those three weeks Alina would turn into the most sour little girl her small town had ever seen.
